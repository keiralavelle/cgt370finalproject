Global Mental Health Crisis

Mental health is a growing global concern, but its causes and impacts are far from uniform. Across countries, mental health outcomes are influenced by a complex mix of social, economic, and technological factors. From access to healthcare and government policy to internet connectivity and daily social media use. These differences create distinct patterns in how anxiety, depression, suicide rates, and broader crisis indicators appear around the world. This data story explores those patterns through a global lens, revealing how mental health challenges vary across regions and populations.

By examining relationships between digital habits, healthcare resources, and crisis scores, the dashboard uncovers both expected and surprising trends. Through interactive visualizations, this dashboard invites you to investigate how mental health conditions differ by country, region, income group, and policy status. You can filter the data to compare nations, identify differences, and better understand the global landscape of mental health. Together, these insights provide a clearer picture of how societal structures and modern lifestyles influence one of the most pressing public health challenges of our time.
